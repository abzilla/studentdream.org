We Produce

Productions that create an incredibly diverse, wildly talented, and highly impactful generation of entrepreneurs.

Startup Community

We believe that the biggest driver of entrepreneurial success is collaboration via community. As an entrepreneur, you CANNOT do everything by yourself. With this in mind we produce meetups, events, and hangouts that create a smart, healthy, and globally diverse startup community.

Startup Skills Training

Enthusiasm with knowledge is the core value that drives our focus on Startup Skills Training. With an emphasis on experiential education, we produce classes and workshops taught by pracitioners and industry leaders that give students the fundamental skills needed to launch and sustain startups. These include

  1. Business Model Basics
  2. Legal Formation
  3. Web Development
  4. The Art of Storytelling
  5. Product and Design
  6. Crowd Funding 101
  7. Technical Marketing
  8. Smart and Healthy Leadership
  9. The Art of Networking
  10. Sales and Business Development

The Student Dream Accelerator

10 wildly talented teams +10 high-impact ideas +10 Empowering Weeks = 10 Launched Startups

The Student Dream accelerator will open its doors beginning in the Summer of 2014. With a mission of launching startups founded by collegiate women, black, and latin students, the accelerator will talk 10 highly talented teams, their ten high-impact tech/new media driven ideas and spend ten weeks equipping them with the startup skills training, network, and resourcesneeded to build, prototype, fund, launch, and sustain their ventures.

Apprenticeship

Student Dream apprenticeships connect student entrepreneurs with seasoned entrepreneurs in the student's industry focus. Mentors guide student teams throughout all stages of their businesses development by opening their company's doors to each startup team. Mentees work on short-term projects that expose them to the functional operations of their industry.

Impact

As of 2011, Latinos and Blacks founded only 1% of technology startups. Additionally, only 2% and 1% of Latinos and Blacks receiving venture capital funding.
